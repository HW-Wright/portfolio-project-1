Why we need to protect the bees!
  • Food

    • One out of every three mouthfuls of our food depend on pollinators such as bees. Crops that depend on pollination are five times more valuable than those that do not.

  • Biodiversity

    • Almost 90% of wild plants and 75% of leading global crops depend on animal pollination.

  • Wildlife

    • Bees are responsible for the pollination of species of trees and plants that, although not edible, provide a habitat to other creates invaluable to the ecosystem.

  • Innovation

    • As a side product to its natural lifecycle, a colony of bees produce sustainable materials that can be used in our everyday life; such as glue.

  • Honey

    • Without a doubt the most popular and valuable material bees produce naturally is honey! It is sustainable and has many benefits to people all over the world. This we will discuss in the section below.

All about the honey!

Studies suggest that honey might offer antidepressant, anticonvulsant, and anti-anxiety benefits. In some studies, honey has been shown to help prevent memory disorders. Wound care. Topical use of medical-grade honey has been shown to promote wound healing, particularly in burns.

Honey has a lower GI value than sugar, meaning that it does not raise blood sugar levels as quickly. Honey is sweeter than sugar, so you may need less of it, but it does have slightly more calories per teaspoon so it's wise to keep a close eye on your portion sizes.
